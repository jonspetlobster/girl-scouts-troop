🌼 Meeting 1 — Welcome to Girl Scouts!

Meeting 1IcebreakersPromise Center

March 12, 2026 • 70 minutes • Theme: "Welcome to Girl Scouts!"

Supplies Needed

  • American flag (small tabletop flag)
  • Name tags + markers
  • Printed Girl Scout Promise (poster or whiteboard)
  • Blue Promise Center petal iron-ons (1 per girl)
  • Construction paper (blue, green, white), scissors, glue sticks, markers/crayons
  • Ball or beanbag for icebreaker
  • Snack

Meeting Plan

0:00–0:10 | Arrival & Name Tags (10 min)

  • Girls make decorated name tags as they arrive
  • Sit in a circle; play music or let them color while waiting
  • Leader split: One greets at door/talks to parents, other sits with arriving girls

0:10–0:15 | Opening Ceremony (5 min)

  • Welcome everyone by name
  • Introduce yourselves
  • "Today is your very first Girl Scouts meeting!"
  • Show the American flag; explain we always start with it

0:15–0:25 | Icebreaker — Name Ball Toss (10 min)

  • Sit in a circle
  • Toss a ball to someone, say their name
  • Go around until everyone has been named
  • Then: toss + say one thing you love (favorite food, animal, color)

0:25–0:35 | Girl Scout Promise (10 min)

  • Show the Promise on poster/whiteboard
  • Read it together slowly, twice
  • Explain what "honor" means in kid terms: keeping your word
  • Give each girl the Promise Center petal to hold (don't iron yet — take home)

0:35–0:50 | Daisy Flower Craft (15 min)

  • Make a paper Daisy: yellow center + colored petals
  • Each petal = a Girl Scout value (keep it simple: "being kind," "helping others," etc.)
  • Write one kind thing they'll do this week on their center

0:50–1:00 | Snack + Free Time (10 min)

  • Casual snack time
  • Let them show each other their flowers

1:00–1:10 | Closing Ceremony (10 min)

  • Friendship squeeze in a circle (hold hands, squeeze passes around)
  • Remind them of next meeting date
  • Each girl takes home: name tag, Daisy flower, Promise Center petal

Notes

  • Keep energy high — first impressions matter
  • If a girl is shy, don't force participation; let them warm up
  • Parents may linger at dropoff — have a helper manage the door
