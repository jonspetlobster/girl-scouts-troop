👨‍👩‍👧 Parent Meeting Guide

March 5, 2026 • 60–75 minutes

This is your first impression. Keep it warm, organized, and short. Parents should leave feeling excited, not overwhelmed.

📋 Before the Meeting

  • Reserve your meeting space
  • Print sign-in sheet (name, girl's name, email, phone, allergies/needs)
  • Print a one-page handout with: meeting schedule, what to bring, contact info
  • Have a snack sign-up sheet ready
  • Bring a sample Daisy vest/tunic to show
  • Have the troop calendar (March–June) printed or ready to share

📝 Meeting Agenda

0:00 – 0:10 (10 min)

Welcome & Introductions

Greet families as they arrive. Once settled:

  • Introduce yourselves: "Hi, I'm Megan and this is Sasha. We're co-leading this troop and we're so excited to get started."
  • Quick parent introductions: name, daughter's name, one thing she's excited about
"We're both first-time leaders, and we want to be upfront about that. We've done our homework, we have great resources from Girl Scouts, and we're going to figure this out together. What we lack in experience, we make up for in enthusiasm."
0:10 – 0:20 (10 min)

What Is Girl Scouts? (The Real Version)

"Girl Scouts isn't just about cookies — though those are fun too. It's about giving girls a space where they can try new things, build confidence, and learn to be kind leaders. At the Daisy level, that looks like crafts, games, outdoor adventures, simple science experiments, and learning about being a good friend and community member."

Key points to cover:

  • It's girl-led — even at age 6, the girls make choices about what they do
  • It's about the journey, not collecting badges — badges give us structure, but the real goal is the experience
  • Daisy petals teach the Girl Scout Law — values like honesty, courage, kindness, and responsibility
  • GSUSA has been doing this since 1912 — there are proven, tested activity plans for everything
0:20 – 0:35 (15 min)

Our Troop Vision

"Here's what we want this troop to be about. We have big goals, but we're going to keep it manageable and fun."

Share your vision pillars:

  1. 🤝 Kind & Respectful: Girls learn to be good friends, listen to each other, and treat everyone with respect. This is the foundation — the petals help us teach this naturally.
  2. 💪 Leadership & Confidence: Even small things — leading the pledge, choosing an activity, speaking up — build confidence at this age.
  3. 🏕️ Adventure: Getting outside, trying new things, going on field trips. We'll aim for at least one outdoor adventure this spring.
  4. 🔬 Science & Engineering: Simple STEM activities — building bridges from popsicle sticks, planting seeds, basic experiments. Girl Scouts has great badges for this.
  5. 🎨 Crafts & Creativity: Every meeting will include something hands-on. Messy is okay.
  6. 😄 Fun: If the girls aren't having fun, we're doing it wrong. That's our north star.
0:35 – 0:50 (15 min)

What to Expect

Cover the practical stuff:

  • Meeting schedule: Weekly/biweekly, [day] at [time], [location]. First meeting March 12.
  • Meeting length: About 60–75 minutes.
  • What girls wear: Daisy tunic or vest (available at gsstore.org, ~$16–20). They'll add petals and badges throughout the year.
  • Dues: $[amount] per semester covers supplies and badges. Cookie money helps fund field trips and special activities.
  • Communication: We'll use [email/text/app] for updates and reminders.
  • What to bring: Girls just need to show up ready to have fun. We provide supplies.
  • Allergies/needs: Please share any allergies, medical needs, or behavioral things we should know. We want every girl to feel safe and included.
💡 Talking point: "We're starting mid-year, which is totally normal. We won't earn every badge this year, and that's fine. Our goal is to build a strong foundation and have a blast. The girls will earn 6-8 petals by June — that's a lot to be proud of."
0:50 – 1:05 (15 min)

How Parents Can Help

"Running a troop takes a village. We're the leaders, but we absolutely need your help. Here are some easy ways to pitch in — none of them require being an expert in anything."
  • 🍪 Snack rotation: Sign up for one meeting to bring a simple snack (we have an allergy list)
  • 🚗 Field trip drivers: When we do outings, we'll need extra drivers
  • 🎨 Meeting helpers: Extra hands during craft time are always welcome — just show up
  • 📧 Communication helper: Someone who's good at reminders/organizing
  • 💰 Treasurer: Track our small budget, collect dues (if someone's good with spreadsheets)
  • 📦 Supply shopper: Occasionally pick up craft supplies from a list we provide
  • 🏕️ Special skills: Do you garden? Do art? Work in science? We'd love to bring parents in for special activities

Pass around a sign-up sheet with these roles. Don't pressure — make it easy and low-commitment.

1:05 – 1:15 (10 min)

Q&A and Wrap-Up

Open the floor for questions. Common ones:

  • "What if my daughter doesn't like it?" — Totally fine! Give it 3 meetings. No pressure.
  • "Do I need to stay during meetings?" — For Daisies, parents usually drop off. But you're always welcome to stay or volunteer.
  • "What about cookies?" — Cookie season is typically Jan–March (we may catch the tail end or participate next year). It's optional and fun.
  • "How much does this cost?" — GSUSA registration (~$25/year) + uniform (~$16-20) + troop dues. Financial assistance is available through the council.

End with:

"Thank you all so much for being here. We can't wait to meet your girls on March 12. It's going to be a great spring. If you think of questions later, don't hesitate to reach out."

📄 Handout to Send Home

Create a simple one-pager (or email) with:

  • Troop meeting schedule (dates, time, location)
  • Leader contact info
  • What to bring to first meeting (nothing special, just your daughter!)
  • Where to buy the Daisy tunic/vest
  • Dues amount and when they're due
  • Snack sign-up link/sheet
  • Forms needed: GSUSA registration, emergency contact, photo permission
