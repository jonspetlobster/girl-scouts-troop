👨‍👩‍👧 Parent Meeting Guide

March 5, 2026 • 60–75 minutes

Before the Meeting

  • Reserve meeting space
  • Print sign-in sheet (name, girl's name, email, phone, allergies)
  • Print one-page handout with meeting schedule, what to bring, contact info
  • Have snack sign-up sheet ready
  • Bring sample Daisy vest/tunic
  • Print March–June troop calendar

Agenda

1. Welcome & Introductions (10 min)

  • Introduce yourselves as co-leaders
  • Be upfront about being first-time leaders
  • Quick parent intros: name, daughter's name, one thing she's excited about

2. What Is Girl Scouts? (10 min)

Key points: - Not just about cookies — it's about confidence, kindness, and trying new things - Girl-led even at age 6 (girls make choices) - About the journey, not collecting badges - Daisy petals teach the Girl Scout Law - GSUSA has been doing this since 1912

3. Our Troop Vision (15 min)

Six pillars: 1. 🤝 Kind & Respectful — Being good friends, listening, treating everyone with respect 2. 💪 Leadership & Confidence — Leading the pledge, speaking up, choosing activities 3. 🏕️ Adventure — Getting outside, trying new things, field trips 4. 🔬 Science & Engineering — Simple STEM activities, building, planting, experiments 5. 🎨 Crafts & Creativity — Hands-on every meeting. Messy is okay. 6. 😄 Fun — If the girls aren't having fun, we're doing it wrong

4. What to Expect (15 min)

  • Schedule: Weekly/biweekly, first meeting March 12
  • Length: 60–75 minutes
  • Uniform: Daisy tunic or vest (~$16–20 from gsstore.org)
  • Dues: $[amount] per semester for supplies and badges
  • Communication: Email/text/app for updates
  • Allergies/needs: Please share all relevant info

5. How Parents Can Help (15 min)

  • 🍪 Snack rotation
  • 🚗 Field trip drivers
  • 🎨 Meeting helpers (extra hands during crafts)
  • 📧 Communication helper
  • 💰 Treasurer
  • 📦 Supply shopper
  • 🏕️ Special skills (gardening, art, science, etc.)

6. Q&A and Wrap-Up (10 min)

Common questions: - "What if my daughter doesn't like it?" — Give it 3 meetings - "Do I need to stay?" — Drop-off is fine, but always welcome to stay - "What about cookies?" — Typically Jan–March; optional and fun - "How much does this cost?" — Registration (~$25/yr) + uniform + dues. Financial assistance available.

Handout to Send Home

  • Meeting schedule (dates, time, location)
  • Leader contact info
  • What to bring to first meeting
  • Where to buy Daisy tunic/vest
  • Dues amount and due date
  • Snack sign-up link
  • Forms needed: GSUSA registration, emergency contact, photo permission

Sources: GSUSA new troop guidelines, Volunteer Toolkit.
