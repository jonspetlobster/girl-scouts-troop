🎉 First Troop Meeting Plan

March 12, 2026 • 70 minutes total

Theme: "Welcome to Girl Scouts!" — Learn the Promise, meet each other, earn the Promise Center

🎒 Supplies Needed

📦 Gather these before the meeting:
  • American flag (small tabletop flag is fine)
  • Name tags + markers
  • Printed Girl Scout Promise (large poster or on a whiteboard)
  • Blue Promise Center petal iron-ons (1 per girl) — from GS Shop
  • Construction paper (blue, green, white), scissors, glue sticks, markers/crayons
  • A ball or beanbag for the icebreaker game
  • Snack (provided by [assigned family])
  • Optional: printout of the Daisy flower with all petal names

📋 Meeting Plan: Minute by Minute

0:00 – 0:10 | Arrival & Name Tags (10 min)

🏷️ Welcome & Setup

As girls arrive, have them:

  1. Make a name tag (pre-cut name tag shapes, let them decorate)
  2. Sit in a circle on the floor

While they arrive, play some upbeat music or let them color. Don't start formal activities until most girls are there.

💡 Leader split: One leader greets at the door and talks to parents. The other sits with arriving girls and helps with name tags.
0:10 – 0:20 | Icebreaker Game (10 min)

🎯 "My Name Is... and I Like..."

Sit in a circle. Roll/toss a ball to each girl:

  1. Girl catches the ball
  2. Says: "My name is [name] and I like [something fun]!"
  3. Everyone says: "Hi, [name]!"
  4. Girl rolls the ball to the next person

Go around twice if time allows. Leaders go first to model it.

"My name is Megan and I like hiking! Now I'm going to roll the ball to someone. When you catch it, tell us your name and something you like. Ready?"
0:20 – 0:35 | The Girl Scout Promise (15 min)

🤞 Learning Our Promise

This is the heart of the first meeting. Keep it interactive, not lecture-y.

Step 1: Introduce the Promise

"Every Girl Scout in the whole world knows the Girl Scout Promise. It's like our team pledge. Today we're going to learn it together, and it's totally okay if it takes a few tries!"

Step 2: Read it together

Show the poster. Read it line by line, having the girls repeat after you:

The Girl Scout Promise
On my honor, I will try:
To serve God and my country,*
To help people at all times,
And to live by the Girl Scout Law.
*Girls may substitute wording as appropriate to their beliefs.

Step 3: The Girl Scout Sign

Show them the Daisy sign: hold up your right hand with three fingers up (like a peace sign plus one more). The three fingers stand for the three parts of the Promise.

Step 4: Practice together

Stand up, make the sign, and say the Promise together 2–3 times. Celebrate! Clap and cheer.

💡 Don't worry if they don't memorize it today. They'll say it at the start of every meeting. By meeting 3, they'll know it by heart.
0:35 – 0:45 | Snack Break (10 min)

🍎 Snack & Chat

Keep it simple. While they eat:

  • Ask: "What are you most excited about in Girl Scouts?"
  • Show them a Daisy vest and the petal spots
  • Briefly explain: "Every petal is a different color and teaches us something important. We're going to earn lots of these!"
0:45 – 1:00 | Daisy Flower Craft (15 min)

🌸 Make Your Own Daisy Flower

Each girl makes a paper Daisy flower that they'll add to throughout the year:

  1. Pre-cut: a blue circle (Promise Center) and a green stem
  2. Girls write their name on the blue center
  3. Glue the center to a piece of white paper, add the stem
  4. Explain: "Each time we earn a petal, you'll add a colored petal around your center!"
  5. Girls can decorate the background with markers

These can be kept in a troop folder or sent home to display.

💡 Promise Center earned! After this meeting, each girl earns the blue Promise Center petal for their vest. You can present these at the end of the meeting or at the start of Meeting 2 for a special ceremony.
1:00 – 1:10 | Closing Circle (10 min)

💫 Friendship Circle & Squeeze

End every meeting the same way (girls love the ritual):

  1. Stand in a circle
  2. Cross right arm over left and hold hands with the people next to you
  3. One leader starts a "friendship squeeze" — a gentle hand squeeze that travels around the circle
  4. When it comes back to the leader, everyone raises their joined hands and says: "Goodnight, Girl Scouts!" (or "See you next time!")

Before the squeeze:

"You did an amazing job today! You learned the Girl Scout Promise, earned your Promise Center, and made a beautiful Daisy flower. Next week, we'll start earning our first petal! Who's excited?"

Remind parents of next meeting date as they pick up.

📝 After the Meeting

  • ✅ Send a quick email/text to parents: "Great first meeting! Girls learned the Promise and made Daisy flowers. Next week: [date, time]."
  • ✅ Note any girls who seemed shy, upset, or especially engaged — helps you plan
  • ✅ Debrief with your co-leader: What worked? What would you change?
  • ✅ Order any petals/badges you'll need for the next few meetings
  • ✅ Take a deep breath — you did it! 🎉

🔄 If Things Go Sideways

Real talk for the first meeting:

  • A girl cries and wants her parent: Totally normal. Comfort her, offer to have her sit near a leader. If a parent is there, let them help. Don't force anything.
  • Girls won't stop talking/running around: Use a quiet signal (clap pattern, raising your hand, or "1-2-3, eyes on me"). Teach it at the start and use it consistently.
  • Craft takes 5 minutes instead of 15: Have a backup — a coloring page, Simon Says, or "Freeze Dance" with music.
  • You don't finish everything: That's fine. The girls had fun. That's the only metric that matters for Meeting 1.
