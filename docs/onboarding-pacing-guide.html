📅 March–June Pacing Guide

Realistic goals for a new troop starting mid-year. 14 meetings, 8 petals, 1–2 badges. You've got this.

🎯 Season Goals (Realistic for a New Troop)

  • Promise Center — earned at Meeting 1
  • 8 of 10 Daisy Petals — one per meeting (some meetings earn a petal, some focus on badges or fun)
  • 1–2 Skill-Building Badges — woven in alongside petals
  • Friendship Circle closing — established as ritual from day one
  • At least 1 outdoor adventure
💡 Why not all 10 petals? With 14 meetings (and some weeks off), you realistically have time for 8 petals plus badge work and fun activities. Trying to cram all 10 plus badges leads to rushing. It's better to do 8 well than 10 poorly. Save the remaining 2 for fall.

🏅 Recommended Badges (Beyond Petals)

These complement the petals without duplicating them. Pick 1–2 for the spring.

🤖 Think Like a Citizen Scientist (Daisy)
3 steps. Simple observation activities — perfect for outdoor meetings. Pairs well with "Use Resources Wisely" petal. Source: GSUSA STEM badge series.
🌱 Eco Friend (Daisy Leaf — Courage)
One of three Daisy "leaves" (Courage, Confidence, Character). Pairs with outdoor activities and the "Use Resources Wisely" petal.
🏗️ Design with Nature (Daisy)
Build something using natural materials. Great for an outdoor meeting. Combines STEM and nature. Source: GSUSA Outdoor badge series.
🎨 Painting (Daisy)
3 steps: explore colors, paint a picture, share your art. Easy to run, always a hit. Good rainy-day option.
🍪 Cookie Business badges
If your troop participates in cookie season, there are specific badges. Otherwise skip for now.
💡 Badge strategy: Don't earn badges separately from petals — weave them in. Example: During the "Use Resources Wisely" (Rosie) petal week, do a nature observation activity and start the Think Like a Citizen Scientist badge. Two birds, one meeting.

Mar 26
Meeting 3: Considerate & Caring (Zinni petal). Activity: make cards for a local nursing home or shelter. Discuss thinking about others' feelings. → Earn: Spring Green Petal

🌸 April — "Courage & Responsibility"

Focus: Build confidence, introduce STEM, try outdoor activities

Apr 2
Meeting 4: Honest & Fair (Lupe petal). Play fair-play games (taking turns, sharing). Activity: "What would you do?" scenarios. → Earn: Light Blue Petal
Apr 9
Meeting 5: Courageous & Strong (Gloria petal). Try something brave — obstacle course, speaking in front of the group. → Earn: Red Petal
Apr 16
Meeting 6: 🔬 STEM Day! Start Think Like a Citizen Scientist badge. Nature walk, observe and draw plants/bugs. Pairs with upcoming "Use Resources Wisely." → Badge work
Apr 23
Meeting 7: Responsible for What I Say & Do (Gerri petal). Discuss responsibility through role-play. Activity: make a "responsibility chart." → Earn: Orange Petal
Apr 30
Meeting 8: 🏕️ Outdoor Adventure! Park meetup. Nature scavenger hunt. Finish Citizen Scientist badge steps. Bring snacks for a "troop picnic." → Earn: Citizen Scientist Badge

🌻 May — "Respect & Community"

Focus: Respect, community service, creativity

May 7
Meeting 9: Respect Myself & Others (Mari petal). Discuss what makes everyone special and different. Activity: self-portrait collage. → Earn: Purple Petal
May 14
Meeting 10: Respect Authority (Tula petal). Talk about listening to teachers, parents, leaders. Activity: "follow the leader" game variations + community helper discussion. → Earn: Magenta Petal
May 21
Meeting 11: Use Resources Wisely (Rosie petal). Recycling activity, make something from recyclables. Discuss not wasting. → Earn: Green Petal
May 28
Meeting 12: 🎨 Creativity Day! Painting badge (if time) or free craft day. Girls choose their activity (girl-led!). → Badge work / Fun day

☀️ June — "Celebration & Looking Ahead"

Focus: Service, sisterhood, celebrate the season

Jun 4
Meeting 13: 🌍 Service Project! Simple community service: pick up litter at a park, make dog toys for a shelter, or collect items for a food bank. Ties to "Make the World a Better Place." → Service hours
Jun 11
Meeting 14: 🎉 End-of-Year Celebration! Bridging/investiture ceremony. Award remaining petals & badges. Friendship circle. Invite parents. Cake or special snack. Photo! → Ceremony
💡 Flexibility built in: This calendar has 14 meetings over 14 weeks. If you skip a week (spring break, illness, etc.), you have room. Drop the "Creativity Day" in May or combine two petals into one meeting if needed. The service project and celebration are non-negotiable — those are the highlights.

⚡ Tips for Maximizing Progress While Keeping It Fun

  1. Layer, don't stack. A nature walk can count toward a petal AND a badge. Look for overlaps.
  2. Ceremony matters. When you present a petal, make it a moment. Call each girl's name, have her walk up, everyone claps. They'll beam.
  3. Don't save all petals for a big ceremony. Award them as earned — the instant gratification keeps motivation high at this age.
  4. Use the VTK plans. GSUSA's Volunteer Toolkit has step-by-step meeting plans for every petal. Use them. Don't reinvent.
  5. The last meeting is sacred. An end-of-year celebration with parents, a bridging/investiture ceremony, and visible recognition of what they earned makes families want to come back in fall.
  6. Photograph everything. Parents love it. Girls love seeing themselves. And it helps you remember what you did.
  7. If a girl misses the petal meeting, she can still earn it. Talk to her about the topic for 5 minutes at the next meeting. Done.
