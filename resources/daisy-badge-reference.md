# Daisy Petal & Badge Reference

*Complete reference for Girl Scout Daisies (ages 6–7, K–1st grade)*

## The Girl Scout Promise
> On my honor, I will try:
> To serve God and my country,*
> To help people at all times,
> And to live by the Girl Scout Law.
>
> *Girls may substitute wording as appropriate to their beliefs.

## The Girl Scout Law
> I will do my best to be
> honest and fair,
> friendly and helpful,
> considerate and caring,
> courageous and strong, and
> responsible for what I say and do,
> and to
> respect myself and others,
> respect authority,
> use resources wisely, and
> make the world a better place, and
> be a sister to every Girl Scout.

## Daisy Petals (10 Total)

Each petal corresponds to one line of the Girl Scout Law:

| Color | Law Line | Flower Friend | Theme |
|-------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Blue (center) | Girl Scout Promise | — | Making & keeping the Promise |
| Light Blue | Honest and Fair | Lupe (Lupine) | Truth-telling, fair play |
| Yellow | Friendly and Helpful | Sunny (Sunflower) | Being a good friend |
| Spring Green | Considerate and Caring | Zinni (Zinnia) | Others' feelings |
| Red | Courageous and Strong | Gloria (Morning Glory) | Bravery, trying new things |
| Orange | Responsible for What I Say and Do | Gerri (Geranium) | Owning mistakes |
| Purple | Respect Myself and Others | Mari (Marigold) | Self-respect, respecting differences |
| Magenta | Respect Authority | Tula (Tulip) | Listening to leaders |
| Green | Use Resources Wisely | Rosie (Rose) | Conservation, not wasting |
| Rose | Make the World a Better Place | Cora (Clover) | Community service |
| Violet | Be a Sister to Every Girl Scout | Vi (Violet) | Sisterhood, inclusion |

## Daisy Leaves (3 Total)

Earned through the "Welcome to the Daisy Flower Garden" Journey:
- **Courage** — Try new things even when they're hard
- **Confidence** — Feel good about what you can do
- **Character** — Do the right thing even when no one is watching

## Daisy Journey Awards
- **Welcome to the Daisy Flower Garden** — The main Daisy journey; introduces the flower garden characters and the Girl Scout Law
- **Between Earth and Sky** — Nature-focused journey
- **5 Flowers, 4 Stories, 3 Cheers for Animals!** — Animal care journey

## How Petals Are Earned

Each petal typically requires completing 3 activities related to that value. The GSUSA Volunteer Toolkit (VTK) provides step-by-step meeting plans for each petal. Activities include:
- Story or discussion about the value
- An active game or role-play
- A craft or creative activity
- A "take action" component (especially for older petals)

Most petals can be earned in 1 meeting, though some leaders spread them across 2.
