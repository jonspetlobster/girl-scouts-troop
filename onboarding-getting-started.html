📖 Getting Started Guide

For first-time leaders of Girl Scout Daisies (ages 6–7, K–1st grade)

What Is a Daisy Troop?

Daisies are the youngest Girl Scouts — kindergartners and first graders (ages 5–7). Named after Juliette Gordon Low's nickname "Daisy," the founder of Girl Scouts in 1912. Your troop of 8 girls is a perfect size: small enough to know every girl, big enough for team activities.

Key insight: At this age, the girls don't care about badges. They care about belonging, having fun, and doing cool stuff. Badges are your framework, not theirs.

What "Girl-Led" Means for 6-Year-Olds

GSUSA's core philosophy is "girl-led." For Daisies, it looks different than for older girls.

Girl-Led at the Daisy Level Means:

  • Choices, not planning. "Should we do the craft first or the game first?"
  • Voting with their feet. If an activity isn't working, pivot.
  • Simple decision-making. Give them 3 options, they pick 2.
  • Sharing what they like. "What was your favorite part?"
  • Taking small roles. Flag holder, leading the Promise, etc.

What It Does NOT Mean:

  • Girls planning meetings or setting agendas
  • Girls choosing all activities
  • Letting chaos reign
  • Feeling guilty about providing structure

The formula: You set the structure. You offer choices within that structure. The girls make those choices. That's girl-led for Daisies.

Meeting Structure That Works

Every Daisy meeting follows the same rhythm (60–75 min total):

  1. Opening (5–10 min): Gather, flag ceremony or Promise, attendance
  2. Activity 1 (15–20 min): Main badge/petal activity
  3. Snack Break (10 min): Rotating snack family each week
  4. Activity 2 (15–20 min): Craft, game, or continuation
  5. Closing (5–10 min): Friendship circle, Friendship Squeeze, announcements

Essential Supplies

  • Daisy vest or tunic (~$16–20 from gsstore.org)
  • Petal and badge iron-ons
  • Small American flag
  • Basic craft supplies: markers, glue sticks, construction paper, scissors
  • First aid kit
  • Roster and emergency contact info
  • Snack sign-up sheet

Keeping It Fun

For the Girls:

  • Alternate between active, crafty, and learning activities
  • Build in movement every 15 minutes
  • Learn 2–3 Girl Scout songs
  • Include outdoor time when possible
  • Celebrate every petal earned

For You:

  • Prep takes 30 min, not 3 hours — use VTK meeting plans
  • Lower your standards lovingly — messy crafts are the point
  • Delegate to parents
  • You don't need to earn every badge
  • Bad meetings happen — move on

Key GSUSA Resources

  • Volunteer Toolkit (VTK) — Complete meeting plans for every petal and badge
  • Badge Explorer — Browse all Daisy badges
  • Girl Scout Shop — Uniforms, badges, supplies
  • Your council website — Local events, training, camping
  • "Welcome to the Daisy Flower Garden" Journey book
