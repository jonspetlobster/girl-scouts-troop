🤝 Co-Leader Coordination Guide

For Megan & Sasha — splitting the work, staying in sync, not burning out

Why Co-Leading Works

  • Backup — troop meets even if one is sick
  • Better ratios — 2 adults for 8 girls = 1:4
  • Shared brain — better ideas, fewer blind spots
  • Sanity — halving work means you enjoy this

Suggested Role Split

Area Leader A Leader B
Meeting planning Odd weeks (1,3,5...) Even weeks (2,4,6...)
Leading meeting Leads their planned week Supports (behavior, crafts)
Parent communication Writes emails/texts Reviews before sending
Supplies Shops for their meetings Shops for their meetings
Money/dues Collects and tracks Reviews
Badge ordering — Orders from GS Shop
Attendance Takes attendance —
Council liaison — Handles council forms

Alternating model: Each leader "owns" every other meeting. Owner plans, preps, leads. Other shows up and supports. Heavy prep only every other week.

Communication Cadence

Weekly Check-in (15 min, Sunday/Monday)

  • What's the plan this week?
  • What supplies needed?
  • Anything to know? (absences, concerns)

Post-Meeting Debrief (5 min, right after)

  • What went well?
  • What to change?
  • Any girl needing extra attention?
  • Supplies to restock?

Monthly Planning (30 min)

  • Next 4 meetings: which petals/badges?
  • Special events or field trips?
  • Budget check
  • Parent volunteer needs?

During Meetings

Lead Leader (planned this meeting):

  • Runs agenda, leads activities, gives instructions
  • Manages transitions
  • Presents petals/badges

Support Leader:

  • Manages behavior (redirects without interrupting)
  • Helps with craft setup/cleanup
  • Sits with girls needing extra help
  • Handles parent questions at drop-off/pickup
  • Takes photos

Rule: While one talks to the group, the other scans for problems. Never both focused on same thing.

When Things Get Tricky

Disagreements:

  • Never disagree in front of girls/parents
  • Table it: discuss after the meeting
  • Default to planner's choice; adjust next time

One leader absent:

  • Other runs solo (share plans in advance!)
  • Ask parent volunteer to be second adult (required by GSUSA)
  • Simplify the plan

Uneven workload:

  • Bring it up early, not when resentful
  • Be specific: "I've handled X for 3 weeks — can we swap?"
  • Revisit role split monthly

Shared Documents

Keep in one shared place: - ✅ Meeting plans (past and upcoming) - ✅ Attendance roster - ✅ Parent contacts + emergency info - ✅ Budget tracker - ✅ Badge tracking per girl - ✅ Snack rotation calendar

Taking Care of Yourselves

  • "Phone it in" meetings are valid (coloring + free play + closing circle)
  • Lean on VTK meeting plans
  • Celebrate your wins
  • Take breaks when needed — no guilt
  • You don't need to be perfect

Sources: GSUSA co-leader guidelines, Volunteer Toolkit.
