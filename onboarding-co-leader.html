🤝 Co-Leader Coordination Guide

For Megan & Sasha — how to split the work, stay in sync, and not burn out.

💡 Why Co-Leading Works

Co-leading a Daisy troop is ideal. You have:

  • Backup. If one of you is sick, the troop still meets.
  • Better ratios. 2 adults for 8 girls = 1:4. That's manageable.
  • Shared brain. Two people planning means better ideas and fewer blind spots.
  • Sanity. Halving the work means you actually enjoy this instead of dreading it.

The key is clear roles and good communication. Here's how.

📋 Suggested Role Split

Pick the split that matches your strengths. Here's a starting point — adjust as you learn what works.

AreaLeader ALeader B
Meeting planning Plans odd weeks (1, 3, 5...) Plans even weeks (2, 4, 6...)
Leading meeting Leads the meeting they planned Supports (manages behavior, helps with craft)
Parent communication Weekly emails/texts Reviews before sending
Supplies & shopping Shops for their planned meetings Shops for their planned meetings
Money / dues Collects and tracks Reviews / second pair of eyes
Badge ordering — Orders from GS Shop
Attendance & records Takes attendance —
Council liaison — Handles council emails/forms
💡 The alternating model works best: Each leader "owns" every other meeting. The owner plans, preps supplies, and leads. The other shows up and supports. This way you each only do heavy prep every other week.

📞 Communication Cadence

Stay in sync with minimal effort:

🔄 Weekly Check-in (15 min, text or call)

Before each meeting, the leader who's "on" shares their plan with the other. Keep it simple:

  • What's the plan this week?
  • What supplies are needed?
  • Anything I should know? (absent girls, parent concerns, etc.)

When: Sunday or Monday evening for a midweek meeting.

📝 Post-Meeting Debrief (5 min, in person)

Right after each meeting, before you leave:

  • What went well?
  • What should we change?
  • Any girl who needs extra attention next time?
  • Supplies to restock?

📅 Monthly Planning (30 min, coffee or call)

Once a month, look ahead at the next 4 meetings together:

  • Which petals/badges are we covering?
  • Any special events or field trips?
  • Budget check — are we on track?
  • Parent volunteer needs?

⚡ During Meetings: Who Does What

Having two leaders in the room is a superpower. Use it:

Lead Leader (the one who planned this meeting)

  • Runs the agenda — leads activities, gives instructions
  • Manages transitions between activities
  • Presents petals/badges

Support Leader

  • Manages behavior (redirect a distracted girl without interrupting the activity)
  • Helps with craft setup and cleanup
  • Sits with girls who need extra help
  • Handles parent questions at drop-off/pickup
  • Takes photos!
💡 The "one talks, one manages" rule: While one leader is talking to the group, the other is scanning for problems, helping stragglers, and managing the room. Never have both leaders focused on the same thing.

🚨 When Things Get Tricky

If you disagree on something:

  • Never disagree in front of the girls or parents
  • Table it: "Let's talk about this after the meeting"
  • Default to the planner's choice for that meeting — you can adjust next time
  • If it's a values issue, discuss it over coffee, not text

If one leader can't make a meeting:

  • The other runs it solo (this is why you share plans in advance)
  • Ask a parent volunteer to be the second adult (GSUSA requires 2 adults present)
  • Simplify the plan — it's okay to do a "light" meeting

If workload feels uneven:

  • Bring it up early, not when you're resentful
  • Use specific language: "I've been handling X the last 3 weeks — can we swap?"
  • Revisit the role split monthly — what made sense in March may not work in May

🗂️ Shared Tools & Documents

Keep everything in one shared place (Google Drive folder, shared note, or this website):

  • ✅ Meeting plans (past and upcoming)
  • ✅ Attendance roster
  • ✅ Parent contact list + emergency info
  • ✅ Budget tracker (income: dues/cookies; expenses: supplies/badges)
  • ✅ Badge tracking (which girls earned what)
  • ✅ Snack rotation calendar
  • ✅ This onboarding package!

❤️ Taking Care of Yourselves

You're volunteers. This should be fun for you too.

  • It's okay to have a "phone it in" meeting. Coloring pages + free play + Friendship Circle = valid meeting.
  • Lean on the VTK. GSUSA wrote meeting plans so you don't have to start from scratch.
  • Celebrate your wins. You showed up. You led 8 six-year-olds through an activity. That's impressive.
  • If one of you needs a break, take it. The other covers. No guilt.
  • You don't need to be perfect. The girls just need you to be there, be kind, and be organized-ish.
