*March 12, 2026 • 70 minutes • Theme: "Welcome to Girl Scouts!"*

## Supplies Needed
- American flag (small tabletop flag)
- Name tags + markers
- Printed Girl Scout Promise (poster or whiteboard)
- Blue Promise Center petal iron-ons (1 per girl)
- Construction paper (blue, green, white), scissors, glue sticks, markers/crayons
- Ball or beanbag for icebreaker
- Snack

## Meeting Plan

### 0:00–0:10 | Arrival & Name Tags (10 min)
- Girls make decorated name tags as they arrive
- Sit in a circle; play music or let them color while waiting
- **Leader split:** One greets at door/talks to parents, other sits with arriving girls

### 0:10–0:20 | Icebreaker: "My Name Is... and I Like..." (10 min)
- Sit in circle, roll/toss ball
- Girl catches ball, says: "My name is [name] and I like [something]!"
- Everyone: "Hi, [name]!"
- Leaders go first to model

### 0:20–0:35 | The Girl Scout Promise (15 min)
1. **Introduce:** "Every Girl Scout in the whole world knows this Promise."
2. **Read line by line**, girls repeat:

> **The Girl Scout Promise**
> On my honor, I will try:
> To serve God and my country,*
> To help people at all times,
> And to live by the Girl Scout Law.
> *Girls may substitute wording as appropriate to their beliefs.

3. **Teach the Girl Scout sign:** Right hand, three fingers up
4. **Practice together** 2–3 times. Celebrate!

### 0:35–0:45 | Snack Break (10 min)
- Ask: "What are you most excited about in Girl Scouts?"
- Show a Daisy vest and where petals go

### 0:45–1:00 | Daisy Flower Craft (15 min)
- Pre-cut blue circles (Promise Center) and green stems
- Girls write name on blue center
- Glue center to white paper, add stem
- "Each time we earn a petal, you'll add a colored petal around your center!"
- **→ Earn: Promise Center**

### 1:00–1:10 | Closing: Friendship Circle & Squeeze (10 min)
1. Stand in circle
2. Cross right arm over left, hold hands
3. Leader starts a gentle hand squeeze around the circle
4. When it returns, raise hands: "Goodnight, Girl Scouts!"
5. Announce next meeting date

## After the Meeting
- Send quick parent update email
- Note shy/upset/engaged girls
- Debrief with co-leader
- Order petals for next meetings

## If Things Go Sideways
- **Girl cries:** Comfort, offer to sit near leader. Don't force.
- **Girls won't settle:** Use quiet signal (clap pattern, "1-2-3 eyes on me")
- **Craft takes 5 min:** Backup — coloring page, Simon Says, Freeze Dance
- **Don't finish everything:** That's fine. Fun is the only metric for Meeting 1.
